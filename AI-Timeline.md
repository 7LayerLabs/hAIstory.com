# The AI Timeline — From Turing to Today

*A simplified, thorough history of artificial intelligence. Compiled May 2026.*

---

## The Big Picture (TL;DR)

AI has existed as an idea since the 1950s but only "worked" in the way most people now experience it starting around **2017**, when researchers at Google invented the **Transformer** — the architecture behind every modern chatbot. **OpenAI** scaled it up and made it famous with **ChatGPT** in late 2022. **Anthropic**, founded by ex-OpenAI safety researchers, became its main rival with the **Claude** model family. Google, Meta, xAI, and a wave of Chinese labs (especially DeepSeek) are all racing alongside them. The bottleneck is no longer ideas — it's **compute, electricity, and data**. Most major lab leaders now publicly predict transformative AI ("AGI") sometime between **2027 and 2032**.

---

## Era 1 — Foundations (1950s–2011)

The decades where AI was mostly a dream and a research curiosity.

- **1950 — The Turing Test.** Alan Turing proposes the "imitation game": if a machine can convince a human it's human via text, that's intelligence. *Sets the bar for what AI is even trying to do.*
- **1956 — Dartmouth Workshop.** McCarthy, Minsky, and Shannon coin the term "artificial intelligence" at a Dartmouth summer conference. *AI officially becomes a field.*
- **1958 — The Perceptron.** Frank Rosenblatt builds the first "learning machine" — a primitive neural network. *The seed of every modern AI model.*
- **1974–1987 — The AI Winters.** Hype outran reality, funding collapsed twice. *A reminder that AI progress isn't linear.*
- **1997 — Deep Blue beats Kasparov.** IBM's chess computer defeats the world champion. *First time a machine clearly outsmarted humans at a "thinking" task.*
- **2011 — Watson wins Jeopardy!** IBM's Watson beats Ken Jennings. *Showed AI could handle messy human language, not just rules.*

---

## Era 2 — The Deep Learning Revolution (2012–2017)

Neural networks suddenly start working — because GPUs got fast enough.

- **2012 — AlexNet.** Krizhevsky, Sutskever, and Hinton crush the ImageNet image-recognition contest using GPUs. *Triggered the modern deep learning boom. (Sutskever later co-founds OpenAI.)*
- **2013 — Word2Vec.** Google shows that AI can learn relationships like "king − man + woman = queen" from text alone. *Proof that language has math hiding inside it.*
- **2014 — GANs.** Ian Goodfellow invents networks that learn by competing with each other. *Birth of AI-generated images, deepfakes, etc.*
- **2014 — Google buys DeepMind** (London startup). *DeepMind becomes Google's elite AI lab.*
- **2016 — AlphaGo beats Lee Sedol.** DeepMind's Go-playing AI defeats the world champion 4–1. *Cracked a game everyone thought was 10 years out.*
- **June 2017 — "Attention Is All You Need."** Eight Google researchers publish the **Transformer** paper — the architecture behind every chatbot you've ever used. *The single most important paper in modern AI.*

---

## Era 3 — The Transformer & LLM Era (2018–2022)

The Transformer + huge data + huge GPUs = something nobody quite expected.

- **June 2018 — GPT-1** (OpenAI, 117M parameters). *First "Generative Pre-trained Transformer" — proof of concept.*
- **Oct 2018 — BERT** (Google). Powers Google Search. *Made language understanding mainstream.*
- **Feb 2019 — GPT-2** (1.5B parameters). OpenAI delays release citing safety concerns. *First model that wrote scarily coherent text.*
- **2019 — OpenAI restructures** as "capped-profit" and Microsoft invests **$1B**. *The deal that funded the next 5 years of AI.*
- **May 2020 — GPT-3** (175B parameters). *The first model where you could just ask it things and it'd figure out what you meant.*
- **2020 — AlphaFold 2** (DeepMind) solves protein folding, a 50-year-old biology grand challenge. *First time AI made a serious scientific discovery.*
- **June 2021 — GitHub Copilot** launches (powered by OpenAI Codex). *AI starts writing real code professionally.*
- **Apr 2022 — DALL-E 2** (OpenAI). *AI image generation goes mainstream.*
- **Aug 2022 — Stable Diffusion** released free and open by Stability AI. *Image AI escapes the labs — anyone can run it on a laptop.*

---

## Era 4 — The ChatGPT Earthquake (Late 2022 – 2023)

The moment AI stopped being for researchers and became a household word.

- **Nov 30, 2022 — ChatGPT launches.** Free chatbot built on GPT-3.5. **1 million users in 5 days. 100M in 2 months.** *Fastest-growing consumer product in history. Triggers the modern AI gold rush.*
- **Feb 2023 — Meta's LLaMA leaks** onto the internet days after release. *Accidentally launches the open-source AI movement.*
- **Mar 2023 — GPT-4** (OpenAI). Multimodal (text + images), passes the bar exam. *The new ceiling for AI capability.*
- **Mar 2023 — Claude 1** (Anthropic). *Anthropic's first public model. The OpenAI rival is real.*
- **Jul 2023 — xAI founded** by Elon Musk. *He's back in the AI race after leaving OpenAI in 2018.*
- **Jul 2023 — Meta releases LLaMA 2** open-source. *Frontier AI is no longer locked up.*
- **Dec 2023 — Google merges Brain + DeepMind** into one unit. *Google realizes it's losing the race and consolidates.*

## Era 5 — The Two-Horse Race: OpenAI vs. Anthropic

Two labs, founded by people who used to work together, now define the frontier.

### How Anthropic Started

In late 2020, **Dario Amodei** (OpenAI's VP of Research) and his sister **Daniela Amodei**, plus 7 senior OpenAI researchers, **left OpenAI to found Anthropic**. They thought OpenAI was moving too fast and not taking safety seriously. Anthropic's pitch: build AI that's **safe by design**, not safe by patches afterward. Their signature research is **Constitutional AI** (training models against a written set of principles) and **mechanistic interpretability** (literally looking inside the model to see what it's "thinking").

### The Sam Altman Drama (November 2023)

- **Nov 17, 2023:** OpenAI's board abruptly fires Sam Altman, citing lack of candor.
- **Nov 18–19:** Greg Brockman quits in solidarity. Microsoft offers Altman a job.
- **Nov 20:** **~700 of OpenAI's ~770 employees** sign a letter threatening to follow Altman to Microsoft unless the board resigns.
- **Nov 21:** Altman is **rehired**. New board installed (Bret Taylor, Larry Summers, Adam D'Angelo). *The closest thing AI has had to a coup.*

### Other Big OpenAI Departures

- **May 2024 — Ilya Sutskever** (chief scientist, co-founder) leaves to found **Safe Superintelligence Inc.**
- **May 2024 — Jan Leike** (head of alignment) leaves for **Anthropic**, saying safety "took a backseat to shiny products."

### Funding & Valuations (the money is staggering)

- **OpenAI:** $1B (2019) → $13B+ Microsoft total → **$157B (Oct 2024)** → **$300B (Mar 2025)** → **$852B (Mar 2026)**.
- **Anthropic:** Amazon **$8B** total → Google adds **$1B** (Jan 2025, $60B valuation) → Amazon adds up to **$25B** more (Apr 2026, ~$33B total Amazon commitment).
- **Microsoft–OpenAI** is the deepest tech partnership of the decade. **Amazon–Anthropic** is the response.

## Era 6 — The Field Explodes (2023–2026)

The race isn't two horses — it's a stampede.

### Google / DeepMind
- **Bard → Gemini** rebrand (2023–24).
- **Gemini 1.5** (Feb 2024): million-token context.
- **Gemini 2.0** (Dec 2024): "agentic era" model.
- **Gemini 2.5 + Project Astra** (May 2025): live video-aware AI assistant.

### Meta (open-source champion)
- **LLaMA 1** leaks Feb 2023.
- **LLaMA 2** Jul 2023 — official open release.
- **LLaMA 3** Apr 2024, then **3.1 with 405B params** Jul 2024 — first open model rivaling GPT-4.
- **LLaMA 4** (2025): mixture-of-experts architecture.

### xAI (Elon Musk)
- Founded Jul 2023. **Grok 1, 2, 3** released in rapid succession. Built **Colossus**, one of the largest GPU clusters on Earth, in months.

### DeepSeek (the China shock)
- **Dec 2024 — DeepSeek V3** matches GPT-4 quality at a fraction of the cost.
- **Jan 2025 — DeepSeek R1** rivals OpenAI's o1, reportedly trained for ~**$5.6M**. *Crashes US tech stocks for a day. Forces every US lab to rethink efficiency.*

## Era 7 — The Infrastructure Race (2024–2026)

The bottleneck shifts from algorithms to **chips and electricity**.

- **NVIDIA** is the picks-and-shovels winner. H100 GPUs became 2023's most coveted hardware. **Blackwell (B100/B200)** announced Mar 2024 — 2.5× faster than H100.
- **NVIDIA market cap:** ~$1T (2023) → **$4T (Oct 2024)** → **$5T (early 2025).** One of the most valuable companies on Earth.
- **Stargate Project** (announced Jan 2025): OpenAI + Oracle + SoftBank pledge **$500B** over 4 years for AI data centers. First Texas site opened Sept 2025.
- **Energy crunch:** AI's electricity demand is breaking the grid.
  - **Microsoft** signs 20-year deal to **restart Three Mile Island** nuclear reactor (Sept 2024).
  - **Amazon** signs $500M nuclear deal with Talen Energy.
  - **Google, Meta** following with similar nuclear RFPs.

## Era 8 — Where We Are Now (Early 2026)

- **Agentic AI** is the frontier — models that don't just answer, they **do**: browse, write code, control computers, complete multi-step tasks.
- Every major lab (OpenAI Agents SDK, Anthropic Claude Agent SDK, AWS Strands) ships agent frameworks.
- **Reasoning models** (o-series, Claude with extended thinking, Gemini Deep Think) trade more compute-at-runtime for dramatically better answers.
- The **Musk vs. OpenAI lawsuit** is in trial as of late April 2026 — Musk is suing for $134B claiming OpenAI abandoned its nonprofit mission.
- The **"data wall"** debate: we've used most of the high-quality public text on Earth. Synthetic data and better training methods are the new frontier.

## Era 9 — The Future (Projections from the People Building It)

These are predictions from **the actual CEOs**, not pundits. Treat with appropriate skepticism — these people also have funding rounds to raise.

### Sam Altman (OpenAI)
- **2025:** "AI agents join the workforce."
- **2026:** AI systems "discover novel insights."
- **2027:** Real-world humanoid robots.
- **~2032–2035:** Superintelligence ("a few thousand days").

### Dario Amodei (Anthropic)
- In his Oct 2024 essay **"Machines of Loving Grace"**: "powerful AI" could arrive **as early as 2026**.
- Predicts a "compressed 21st century" — AI compresses 50–100 years of biomedical progress into 5–10 years.

### Demis Hassabis (Google DeepMind)
- AGI in **5–10 years (2030–2035).**
- Impact will be "10× the Industrial Revolution, happening 10× faster."
- Believes one or two more breakthroughs (beyond just scaling) are still required.

### Rough Consensus Window

| Timeframe | What likely arrives |
|-----------|---------------------|
| **2025–2026** | Agentic AI in production; reasoning models become standard |
| **2026–2027** | First AI scientific discoveries; real coding autonomy |
| **2027–2030** | Plausible window for AGI (general human-level AI) |
| **2030–2035** | Plausible window for superintelligence (AI beyond humans) |

### The Real Constraints

Five things — not algorithms — will decide how fast this actually happens:
1. **Electricity** (nuclear restarts, grid buildout)
2. **Chip supply** (NVIDIA, TSMC, geopolitics around Taiwan)
3. **Data** (the "data wall" — synthetic data is the bet)
4. **Capital** ($100B+ training runs, trillion-dollar infrastructure)
5. **Regulation** (EU AI Act, US executive orders, China policy)

## A Few Things Worth Remembering

- **The Transformer (2017)** is the single technical breakthrough that made all of this possible. Everything since is scaling, refining, and deploying that one idea.
- **OpenAI and Anthropic share a common ancestor.** Anthropic exists because some of OpenAI's founders thought OpenAI was moving too fast.
- **Open-source is now competitive.** Meta's LLaMA and DeepSeek prove frontier-quality models don't have to be locked up.
- **The bottleneck is physical, not intellectual.** It's electricity, GPUs, and data — not new ideas.
- **Predictions are wider than they look.** Even the optimists privately admit a 5–10 year confidence interval. The real answer to "when AGI?" is "nobody knows, but every serious lab is acting like soon."
