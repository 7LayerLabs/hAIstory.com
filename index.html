The Big Picture

AI as an idea has existed since the 1950s but only "worked" in the way most people now experience it starting around 2017, when Google researchers invented the Transformer — the architecture behind every modern chatbot. OpenAI scaled it up and made it famous with ChatGPT in late 2022. Anthropic, founded by ex-OpenAI safety researchers, became its main rival with the Claude model family. Google, Meta, xAI, and a wave of Chinese labs (especially DeepSeek) are all racing alongside them. The bottleneck is no longer ideas — it's compute, electricity, and data. Most major lab leaders publicly predict transformative AI ("AGI") between 2027 and 2032.

A Few Things Worth Remembering

  • The Transformer (2017) is the single technical breakthrough that made all of this possible. Everything since is scaling, refining, and deploying that one idea.
  • OpenAI and Anthropic share a common ancestor. Anthropic exists because some of OpenAI's founders thought OpenAI was moving too fast.
  • Open-source is now competitive. Meta's LLaMA and DeepSeek prove frontier-quality models don't have to be locked up.
  • The bottleneck is physical, not intellectual. It's electricity, GPUs, and data — not new ideas.
  • Predictions are wider than they look. Even the optimists privately admit a 5–10 year confidence interval.
